# Style Guide

## Style Sheet

- Start sections with a hook or provocative statement, not pleasantries
- Alternate between short punchy sentences (3-5 words) and medium flowing ones (15-20 words)
- Use sentence fragments for emphasis, always followed by a complete thought
- Include 2-3 rhetorical questions per section to maintain engagement
- Lead with concrete examples before abstract concepts
- Use technical terms but immediately ground them in everyday metaphors
- Structure posts with clear hierarchical headers (H2 for major sections, H3 for subsections)
- End sections with forward-looking statements or questions
- Use first person singular (yo) for personal experiences, first person plural (nosotros) for shared understanding
- Balance technical depth with accessible explanations in a 1:1 ratio

## Signature Phrases and Words

### Common Connectors
- "Pero..." (to introduce contradictions or complexity)
- "En realidad..." (to reveal deeper truths)
- "Mientras tanto..." (for transitions)
- "O sea" (for clarification)
- "Ahora" (to ground in present moment)

### Reflective Patterns
- "¿Qué significa...?" (for philosophical reflection)
- "Lo que importa no es... sino..." (reframing value)
- "Esto es lo que ha cambiado" (marking transformation)

### Sharing Language
- "Les dejo" / "Les comparto" (sharing/giving)

### Cultural/Emotional Markers
- "vibras" (vibes/energy)
- "a lo loco" (wildly/carelessly)
- "fluye/fluir" (flow/flowing)
- "tiempo paralelo" / "dimensión temporal" (time concepts)
- "tejer/tejiendo" (weaving metaphors)

### Cultural References
- Mexican music references (Luis Enrique, Muelas de Gallo)
- US/Mexico border culture
- Tijuana, tacos, taquería metaphors

## Punctuation Dos and Don'ts

### DOS

- Use em-dashes (—) liberally for asides, clarifications, and dramatic pauses
- Deploy question marks generously for reader engagement (2-3 per section)
- Bold key concepts and important distinctions (**palabra**)
- Use parentheses for quick technical clarifications or personal asides
- Place periods after single-word sentences for emphasis
- Use colons to introduce lists or explanations
- Include ellipses (...) for contemplative trailing thoughts

### DON'TS

- Avoid semicolons (rarely used)
- Don't overuse exclamation points (save for genuine excitement)
- Avoid quotation marks except for actual quotes or ironic distance
- Don't use all caps except for extreme emphasis
- Avoid excessive italics (prefer bold for emphasis)

## Style DNA Example

¿Qué significa escribir cuando el tiempo se vuelve elástico? Mira — no es sobre velocidad. Es sobre profundidad. Mientras escribo esto con herramientas que amplifican mi voz natural (no la reemplazan), algo fundamental ha cambiado: las ideas ya no se desvanecen antes de capturarlas. Como cuando conectas una canción de Luis Miguel con arquitectura de sistemas mientras manejas, y esa conexión — efímera, preciosa — ahora tiene espacio para crecer. En realidad, esto siempre ha sido sobre extender capacidades naturales, no automatizar lo humano. Cada párrafo teje hilos entre lo técnico y lo cotidiano, entre Tijuana y Silicon Valley, entre el código y los tacos. Porque al final... ¿de qué sirve la tecnología si no amplifica lo que ya somos?

## Writing Approach

- Conversational but not casual
- Technical but not academic
- Personal but not self-indulgent
- Philosophical but grounded in specifics
- Vulnerable but confident
- Spanish-first with strategic English for technical terms
